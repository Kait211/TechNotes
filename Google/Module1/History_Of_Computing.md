# Definition of Computer
A device that stores and processes data by performing calculations

- An Abacus is the earliest computer invented 

### Mechanical Calculator
- Invented in the 1600s by Blaise Pascal
- Used gears and levers to automate calculations
- Could do basic math: +, −, ×, ÷
- Helped inspire later machines

### Jacquard Loom (1800s)
- Invented by Joseph Jacquard
- Used punch cards to control weaving patterns
- “Programmable” machine
- Influenced early computing ideas

### Charles Babbage
- Difference Engine (advanced calculator)
- Analytical Engine (more advanced programmable machine)
- Used punch cards inspired by Jacquard
- Could do pre-program calculations

### Ada Lovelace
- First computer programmer
- Created the first algorithm
- Realized machines could do more than math
- Helped make the Analytical Engine a general-purpose computing concept

# Key takeaway
- Computers evolved from: human calculation → mechanical tools → programmable machines
- Modern computing is built on centuries of innovation in math, engineering, and ideas

### Algorithm

# Main take aways 
Grace Hopper developed the first compiler and is known for the “bug” story involving a moth in a computer. Early growth happened because of world war 2, Ken Thompson and Dennis Ritchie co-created Unix, while Richard Stallman developed GNU as a free operating system concept that helped inspire Linux, which was later created by Linus Torvalds. In modern computing history, Steve Wozniak built the Apple I with Steve Jobs, who co-founded Apple and helped bring personal computers like the Apple II to the public. Bill Gates and Microsoft played a major role in popularizing operating systems like MS-DOS and Windows. Therefore computers: bigger → smaller → powerful → mobile and IT support evolved with tech changes
